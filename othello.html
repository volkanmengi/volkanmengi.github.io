'Ó, te LÓ!'

solo exhibition,  2B Galéria, Budapest

2019. 03. 06. - 2019. o3. 22.

This series of black and white photos was taken around 1984. This is an Old Story (the story of Othello and Desdemona) - relatively Old. Although it does not really represent the whole Old Story, only one moment of the story.

Othello arrives ace from a battle as the enemy fleet is destroyed by the storm. In my transcript, the return of the Commander-in-Chief is accompanied by a storm, thunder, lightning, torrential rain and an earthquake, which is truly exaggerated. Venice is transformed into New York with a surprising experience. During the storm, the skyscrapers wave as a result of the earthquake. In fact, there was no climate change in the old days, if we sighed at all, but there were storms and these storms were always only when they should be. But never in the winter, for example. In the past, when we were telling old stories, we didn't think that on the road to gaining power, we were unable to spot something like a tiny bit of climate change. When the fight, the battle ends,  the Moore has done his job and can go on and have his private life. In other words, not only does it fail to notice climate change, but also was coned by an ugly trick. To be sure, he was, in fact, entangled, tricked, raptured by the fact that the woman he loves actually loves him, and not the other way around, as a shaky woman who bends like a branch - she cheats.

The characters in the photo series wear  costumes some sort of memento or, lets say a  'magnificent' historical costumes,of unknown origin.

The fate of the animation based on the series of photos is also mysterious, and there are chances that it may be presented to the audience.
